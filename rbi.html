RBI & FEMA Compliance

RBI Trade Compliance Services revolve around monitoring and regulating India’s cross-border transactions under FEMA, 1999. The Reserve Bank of India enforces compliance through digital platforms like EDPMS (Export Data Processing and Monitoring System) and IDPMS (Import Data Processing and Monitoring System), ensuring transparency, timely forex realization, and prevention of fraud.

Core RBI Trade Compliance Services

1. Regulatory Framework

• Governed by Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999.
• Updated under Foreign Exchange Management (Export and Import of Goods and Services) Regulations, 2026.
• Provides consolidated rules for exporters, importers, and Authorised Dealer (AD) banks.

2. Digital Monitoring Systems

• EDPMS (Exports): Tracks export transactions ensuring forex proceeds are realized within prescribed timelines.
• IDPMS (Imports): Monitors import payments and validates that goods/services are received against remittances.
• Both systems integrate with Customs ICEGATE and AD banks for real-time compliance.

3. Authorised Dealer (AD) Banks’ Role

• AD banks act as compliance intermediaries.
• They verify documents, report transactions to RBI, and ensure exporters/importers meet FEMA requirements.
• Under 2026 regulations, AD banks have greater flexibility to provide faster services.

RBI Trade Compliance Workflow

Step 1 – Shipping bill filed at Customs (Export) / Bill of entry filed at Customs (Import).
Step 2 – Data flows into EDPMS (Exports) / Data flows into IDPMS (Imports).
Step 3 – AD bank verifies forex realization (Exports) / AD bank verifies payment remittance (Imports).
Step 4 – RBI monitors compliance timelines and transaction status.
Step 5 – Non-compliance is flagged for penalties.

Risks of Non-Compliance

• Delayed forex realization – Exporters may face penalties and restrictions.
• Mismatch in import remittances – Customs clearance issues and RBI scrutiny may arise.
• Penalties under FEMA – Monetary fines, compounding proceedings, and reputational damage.
