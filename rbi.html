RBI & FEMA Compliance

RBI trade compliance services revolve around monitoring and regulating India’s cross-border transactions under FEMA, 1999. The Reserve Bank of India enforces compliance through digital platforms like EDPMS (Export Data Processing and Monitoring System) and IDPMS (Import Data Processing and Monitoring System), ensuring transparency, timely forex realization, and prevention of fraud.


Core RBI Trade Compliance Services

1. Regulatory Framework

  • Governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999.
  • Updated under the Foreign Exchange Management (Export and Import of Goods and Services) Regulations, 2026.
  • Provides consolidated rules for exporters, importers, and Authorised Dealer (AD) banks.

2. Digital Monitoring Systems

  • EDPMS (Exports) – Tracks export transactions and ensures forex proceeds are realized within prescribed timelines.
  • IDPMS (Imports) – Monitors import payments and verifies that goods/services are received against remittances.
  • Both systems integrate with Customs ICEGATE and Authorised Dealer Banks for real-time compliance monitoring.

3. Role of Authorised Dealer (AD) Banks

  • AD banks act as compliance intermediaries.
  • They verify documents, report transactions to RBI, and ensure exporters/importers meet FEMA requirements.
  • Under the 2026 regulations, AD banks have greater flexibility to provide faster services.

RBI Trade Compliance Workflow

Step Export Transactions Import Transactions
1 Shipping bill filed at Customs Bill of entry filed at Customs
2 Data flows into EDPMS Data flows into IDPMS
3 AD bank verifies forex realization AD bank verifies payment remittance
4 RBI monitors compliance timelines RBI monitors receipt of goods/services
5 Non-compliance flagged for penalties Non-compliance flagged for penalties

Risks of Non-Compliance

  • Delayed forex realization – Exporters may face penalties and restrictions.
  • Mismatch in import remittances – Can lead to customs clearance issues and RBI scrutiny.
  • Penalties under FEMA – Includes monetary fines, compounding proceedings, and reputational damage.
